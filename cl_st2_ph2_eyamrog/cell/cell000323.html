    Summary

    Human cytomegalovirus (HCMV) infects the majority of the human population and represents the leading viral cause of congenital birth defects. HCMV utilizes the glycoproteins gHgLgO (Trimer) to bind to platelet-derived growth factor receptor alpha (PDGFRα) and transforming growth factor beta receptor 3 (TGFβR3) to gain entry into multiple cell types. This complex is targeted by potent neutralizing antibodies and represents an important candidate for therapeutics against HCMV. Here, we determine three cryogenic electron microscopy (cryo-EM) structures of the trimer and the details of its interactions with four binding partners: the receptor proteins PDGFRα and TGFβR3 as well as two broadly neutralizing antibodies. Trimer binding to PDGFRα and TGFβR3 is mutually exclusive, suggesting that they function as independent entry receptors. In addition, Trimer-PDGFRα interaction has an inhibitory effect on PDGFRα signaling. Our results provide a framework for understanding HCMV receptor engagement, neutralization, and the development of anti-viral strategies against HCMV.

    The overall structure of the HCMV Trimer gHgLgO adopts a boot-like architecture with approximate dimensions of 170 Å in length and 70 Å in width (Figure 1B). The three subunits interact in a linear order, where the gL subunits bridge the gH and gO subunits in the center of the complex (Figures 1A and 1B). The electrostatic surface charge is distributed asymmetrically across the gHgLgO complex with a negative charge cluster at the proximal viral membrane (VM) gH region and a positive charge cluster at the distal VM gO region of the complex (Figure 1C). Similarly, the 19 observed N-linked glycosylation sites are distributed asymmetrically along the gHgLgO complex with 5 on gH, 1 on gL, and 13 on gO. This remarkable enrichment of glycosylated residues at the distal gO region is particularly striking along the back side of complex (Figure 1D). In contrast, the front side of the trimer complex appears to lack glycosylated residues in all three subunits (Figure 1D). Such asymmetric distribution of surface charge and glycosylation will have important implications for the receptor interactions.

    Protein expression and purification

    Biolayer interferometry

    The interactions between the PDGFRα proteins and the HCMV Trimer (WT and mutant) were analyzed by biolayer interferometry using an Octet Red96 system (ForteBio). All data were collected at 25°C using 96-well black flat bottom plates (Greiner Bio-One). For calculation of binding affinities, recombinant PDGFRα proteins were captured onto anti human Fc-coated (AHC) sensors (Sartorius), and tested for binding to the CMV trimer as soluble analyte. Sensors were equilibrated for 10 min before the assay in PBS, following manufacturer’s instructions. All steps, loading, baseline, association and dissociation were performed in PBS.
    For comparison of relative binding between the HCMV trimer and the PDGFRα WT and mutant proteins, the PDGFRα proteins were captured onto AHC sensors, and tested for binding to the trimer as soluble analyte at 50 nM concentration. Binding to PDGFRα mutant proteins was represented relative to trimer binding to the PDGFRα WT protein. Binding units at the end of the association step were used to calculate the relative binding shown in the plot. In all cases, data was acquired using the Octet Red instrument (Forte Pall software version 9.0) and subsequently the Biaevaluation software version 4.1 (GE Healthcare) was utilized for calculations of kinetic parameters. Data were fit to a 1:1 Langmuir binding model.

    PDGFRα activation and signaling and Western Blotting

    The fibroblast cell line MRC-5 was used to study receptor phosphorylation and downstream signaling. MRC-5 were grown in RPMI media supplemented with 10% FBS, glutamine and antibiotics. Cells were cultured at 37°C and 5% CO2. The cells were seeded in M6 well plates, grown to ∼75% confluency and starved O/N prior to stimulation. The day of the assay, cells were stimulated with PDGF-AA (3.7 nM concentration), CMV trimer, or PDGF-AA:CMV trimer at increasing molar ratios. Stimulations were performed at 37°C for 10 minutes in serum free media. Following treatment, the cells were washed with cold PBS and lysed (lysis buffer: 50 mM Tris HCl pH 7.4, 150 mM NaCl, 2 mM EDTA, 1% (v/v) NP40, supplemented with protease (Roche) and phosphatase inhibitors (Sigma)). Samples were diluted in loading buffer (Thermo Fisher Scientific) using denaturing conditions, and analyzed by western blotting using a LI-COR instrument. Cell lysates were centrifuged for 20 minutes at 14,000 rpm and the resulting samples were diluted in reducing SDS-PAGE loading buffer (Thermo Fisher). Samples were subjected to SDS-PAGE using NuPAGE Bis-Tris gels (Thermo Fisher) and imaged using a LI-COR instrument. Western blot images were not processed beyond modification of the image contrast and cropping of the region of interest to facilitate visualization. None of the lanes in the blots were removed, and the aforementioned modifications were applied to the entire image.
