    Non-coding 7S RNA regulates mitochondrial transcription in mammalian cells

    7S RNA binding induces POLRMT dimerization and prevents promoter recognition

    Summary

    The mitochondrial genome encodes 13 components of the oxidative phosphorylation system, and altered mitochondrial transcription drives various human pathologies. A polyadenylated, non-coding RNA molecule known as 7S RNA is transcribed from a region immediately downstream of the light strand promoter in mammalian cells, and its levels change rapidly in response to physiological conditions. Here, we report that 7S RNA has a regulatory function, as it controls levels of mitochondrial transcription both in vitro and in cultured human cells. Using cryo-EM, we show that POLRMT dimerization is induced by interactions with 7S RNA. The resulting POLRMT dimer interface sequesters domains necessary for promoter recognition and unwinding, thereby preventing transcription initiation. We propose that the non-coding 7S RNA molecule is a component of a negative feedback loop that regulates mitochondrial transcription in mammalian cells.

    Cryo-EM structure of POLRMT in complex with 7S RNA

    To elucidate the molecular details of 7S inhibition of POLRMT, we used single-particle cryo-EM to reconstruct full-length POLRMT interacting with 7S RNA. The reconstruction, with an overall resolution of 3.3 Å (gold-standard, FSC 0.143), revealed a homodimer of POLRMT, formed by a “head-to-head” interaction with the two POLRMT molecules angled ∼45° relative to each other as measured from PPR-center of the dimer-PPR (Figures 5A–5D and S4).     Limitations of the study

    Here, we propose that 7S RNA triggers POLRMT dimerization, which further regulates mitochondrial transcription. Although we carried out extensive cryo-EM analysis under a wide range of conditions, we could not obtain a high-resolution map for dimer-bound 7S RNA as it appeared to be conformationally heterogeneous. In addition, we cannot rule out the possibility that other proteins besides mtEXO play a part in regulating POLRMT dimerization in vivo. Future studies should be conducted to determine how the mitochondria transduce the signal from the environmental changes to 7S RNA and further control mitochondrial gene expression.

    Method details

    mtDNA quantification analysis

    Total genomic DNA from HeLa cell pellets was isolated using a Zymo genomic DNA purification Kit (Zymo: D3024) according to the kit instructions. mtDNA copy number was quantified by qPCR with the following reagents: iTaq Universal SYBR Green Supermix (BioRad, 1725121) and the gene specific primers (CytB, Nd6, and 18S, Eurofins genomics). mtDNA levels were assessed using primers at the mitochondrial genes CytB and Nd6; nuclear 18S RNA was used as a loading control. Total genomic DNA (2 μg) from indicated cells was digested using 20 U of BamHI (New England Biolabs) at 37 °C for 1 h and samples were separated in 1% agarose gels. After electrophoresis, DNA was depurinated by incubating agarose gels in 0.25 M HCl for 10 min, washed twice in denaturation buffer (0.5 M NaOH, 1.5 M NaCl) for 30 min, and washed twice in neutralization buffer (0.5 M Tris-HCl pH 7.4, 1.5 M NaCl) for 30 min. DNA was transferred onto a Hybond N+ nitrocellulose membrane overnight, then cross-linked by exposure to 254 nm UV (200 mJ/cm2). Final non-uniform refinement resulted in a reconstruction of overall resolution 3.0 Å (FSC 0.143).
    For the POLRMT mutant structure, POLRMT mutant (2 mg/mL) was mixed with 7S RNA in a 1:3 ratio and incubated on ice for 10 min. 3.5 μL of sample was applied to CryoMatrix® holey grids with amorphous alloy film (R 2/1 geometry; Zhenjiang Lehua Technology Co., Ltd) that had been glow-discharged with 25 mA for 2 min using an EMS 100X (Electron Microscopy Sciences) glow-discharge unit. Grids were then vitrified in a Vitrobot Mk IV (Thermo Fisher Scientific) at 4 °C and 100% humidity (blotted for 8–10 s, blot force 3, 595 filter papers (Ted Pella Inc.)). Cryo-EM data collection was performed as mentioned above. In total, 5,683 movies were collected. Motion correction, CTF-estimation, Fourier cropping (to 1.01 Å/px), particle picking, and extraction (in 300-pixel boxes) (using the pre-trained BoxNet2Mask_20180918 model) were performed on the fly using Warp (see image processing scheme for details). After 2D classification good classes with high resolution features were kept and four ab-initio classes (C1 symmetry) were generated from these particles. This was followed by one round of heterogeneous refinement, taking one good class map and a bad class map and particles from only the good classes. This resulted in a cleaner set of 289,680 particles, and these were used to refine the map further. Final non-uniform refinement (C1 symmetry) of these particles resulted in a map with an overall resolution of 3.24 Å (FSC 0.143).
