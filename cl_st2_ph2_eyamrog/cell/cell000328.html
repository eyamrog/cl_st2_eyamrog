    Summary

    End resection in homologous recombination (HR) and HR-mediated repair of DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) removes several kilobases from 5′ strands of DSBs, but 3′ strands are exempted from degradation. The mechanism by which the 3′ overhangs are protected has not been determined. Here, we established that the protection of 3′ overhangs is achieved through the transient formation of RNA-DNA hybrids. The DNA strand in the hybrids is the 3′ ssDNA overhang, while the RNA strand is newly synthesized. RNA polymerase III (RNAPIII) is responsible for synthesizing the RNA strand. Furthermore, RNAPIII is actively recruited to DSBs by the MRN complex. CtIP and MRN nuclease activity is required for initiating the RNAPIII-mediated RNA synthesis at DSBs. A reduced level of RNAPIII suppressed HR, and genetic loss > 30 bp increased at DSBs. Thus, RNAPIII is an essential HR factor, and the RNA-DNA hybrid is an essential repair intermediate for protecting the 3′ overhangs in DSB repair.

    In this study, we found that RNA polymerase III (RNAPIII) is an essential factor in the HR-mediated repair of DSBs. Moreover, we showed that the formation of RNA-DNA hybrids is an active process and the RNA-DNA hybrid is a repair intermediate in the process of HR and the HR-mediated repair of DSBs. The RNA strand in the hybrids is catalyzed by RNAPIII, and the DNA strand is the 3′-ssDNA overhang. The formation of RNA-DNA hybrids is one of the earliest events in the HR-mediated repair of DSBs.     Genetic loss at DSBs in the siRPC1 or shRPC7-treated cells

    Since the DNA strand in the RNA-DNA hybrids is the 3′ ssDNA overhang, the formation of RNA-DNA hybrids may have a direct effect on the stability of the 3′ ssDNA overhangs during end resection. To verify this, we directly examined whether genetic loss occurs at DSBs when the formation of RNA-DNA hybrids is inhibited. A hygromycinr (hph) gene is located approximately 2,000 bp downstream to the I-SceI cleavage site (Figure 7A). After a DSB is generated by I-SceI cleavage, an extensive end resection, which may still be performed by EXO1 even without RNAPIII-promoted end resection but in a less extent, will remove the 5′ strand of the hphr gene. If the generated 3′-ssDNA overhang is not protected, the cells with partial or total loss of the hphr gene will become sensitive to hygromycin. The results shown in Figures 7B and 7C reveal that approximately 8%–9% of the cells lost the hphr gene in the siRPC1-treated cells, while only ~3% cells lost the hphr gene in the siCtrl-treated cells. In the presence of ML-60218, the loss rate of the hphr gene was about 12%, while in the control cells the loss rate was about 2% (Figure 7C). The rate of hphr gene loss was also measured in the MRN, CtIP, or RPC7 knockdown cells. Like in RPC1 knockdown cells, the RPC7 knockdown cells exhibited about a 13% hphr loss rate, but MRN or CtIP depletion did not increase the hphr loss rate compared to control cells (Figures 7D and 7E).

Once end resection initiates, it will go on to either HR-mediated repair, microhomology-mediated end joining (MMEJ), or abortion of HR-mediated repair; thus, the NHEJ pathway should not be affected. Consistent with the logic, DNA sequencing in both siCtr- or siRPC1-treated cells indicates that almost all break repairs in the category of >30 bp deletion resulted from MMEJ (data not shown), because, once end resection initiates, dsDNA breaks either get unrepaired (HR abortion and cell death) or go to the MMEJ pathway for getting repaired efficiently. Taken together, these results demonstrate that RNAPIII and the formation of RNA-DNA hybrids at DSB site are required for end resection and the protection of the 3′-ssDNA overhangs.

    Discussion

    DNA homologous recombination (HR) and HR-mediated repair of double-strand DNA breaks play a critical role in preserving genome integrity. This biological process consists of end resection, strand invasion and subsequent DNA synthesis, and resolution of Holliday junctions. The molecular mechanisms of strand invasion and resolution of Holliday junctions are relatively well established, largely due to their mechanistic similarity to the same events occurring in prokaryotes.     Capturing RNA-DNA hybrids at DSBs

Purified 3FLAG-HB-6His was mixed with FLAG antibody-affinity gel (Sigma-Aldrich, Cat# A2220) to prepare for the HB column. Extra 3FLAG-HB-6His was washed away with PBS buffer. To prepare genomic DNA from the cells transfected with plasmid expressing restrictive endonuclease I-Sce1, the GAPDH-I-Sce1 U2OS cells were transfected with I-Sce1-expression plasmid for 15 hr. The cells were then collected, washed with PBS twice, and lysed with cold buffer (20 mM HEPES-KOH, pH 7.5, 10 mM KCl, 10 mM EDTA, 0.5% NP-40) on ice for 15 min. Chromatin was collected by centrifugation (1000 g) for 8 min. The chromatin was extracted with a high-salt buffer (HEPES-KOH, pH 7.5, 600 mM KCl, 10 mM EDTA, 0.5% NP-40, 10% glycerol) and subsequently subjected to digestion of proteinase K (1mg/ml) at 37°C for 8-12 hr. DNA was precipitated with ethanol and resuspended in TE buffer. Chromatin DNA was sonicated to an average size of 500 bp. RNaseH-treated or untreated chromatin DNA was subjected protein-RNA-DNA hybrid affinity chromatography with 3FLAG-HB-6His gel. The captured RNA-DNA hybrid-3FLAG-HB-6His gel was incubated at 90°C for 10 min for denaturing the HB domain and releasing RNA-DNA hybrids. Then, Duplex specific nuclease (Evrogen, Cat# EA001) was added and incubated at 70°C for 30 min to digest the DNA strands in the RNA-DNA hybrids. To completely digest DNA, the samples were further digested with DNase I at 37°C for 30 min, followed by heating at 90°C for 10 min to denature these nucleases. Reverse transcription was carried out with designed primers and superscripts-reverse transcriptase (NEB, Cat# M0368S). After incubation at 90°C for 10 min to denature the reverse transcriptase, PCR reactions were carried out with indicated primers. For detecting the formation of RNA-DNA hybrids at the AsisI-mediated break, the experimental method is same as for I-SceI-mediated break. The AsisI-cutting sites chosen in this study were already examined in Jiazhi Hu’s lab at Peking University (unpublished data). We use custom human genome (hg19) sequence for following primer design.

    Immunostaining

    Cover glass plates were soaked in 70% ethanol, followed by flame sterilization and put in a well (6-well plate). After washing the glass plates with PBS twice, a certain amount of cells was inoculated and grew to a log phase. Then, the cells were treated with a chemical (DNA damage agents) for indicated time. Again, cells were washed with PBS to remove chemical, fixed with 4% paraformaldehyde at room temperature for 10 min. The cells, after washing away paraformaldehyde with PBS, were lysed with PBS plus 0.5% Triton X-100 for 10 min, washed with PBS three times, and blocked with a blocking buffer (PBS supplemented with 6% FBS and 1% BSA) at room temperature for 60 min. Then first antibody was added and kept at 4°C for overnight. After washing with PBS to remove first antibody, second antibody in blocking buffer was added and kept at room temperature for 2hrs. After washing with PBS for three times, the glass place with cells on it was upside-down put on a glass slide, sealed with nail polishing oil, and subjected to examination under microscopy after drying.

    Immunoprecipitation assay

    U2OS/293T cells with RPC3-3FLAG-P2A-Neomycin integrated in the RPC3 locus were treated with 5 μM VP16 (Etoposide, Selleck, Cat# S1225) or DMSO.     Chromatin fraction extraction

    U2OS cells were treated with 5 μM VP16 or DMSO for 3 hours. Then, the cells were harvested, washed with ice-cold PBS for 3 times and resuspended in the buffer (20 mM HEPES-KOH pH 7.5, 1 mM EDTA, 10 mM KCl, 0.05% NP-40, 1 mM DTT, protease inhibitor, phosphatase inhibitor mix) for 20 min on ice.     Assay of homologous recombination

    For the RNAi experiment, U2OS DR-GFP cells were first transfected with either RPC1 siRNA or control siRNA. After two days, cells were transfected with an empty vector or an I-SceI expression vector, in which RFP was co-expressed. Cells were harvested two days after the transfection of I-SceI expression plasmid and subjected to flow cytometry analysis to examine GFP signal after DNA double-strand break. Only RFP-positive cells were analyzed for HR efficiency considering possible differences of transfection efficiency. Flowjo software was used to reveal the percentage of GFP-positive cells relative to the number of transfected cells (RFP positive). The data were then compared to a control siRNA treatment in each individual experiment. The dividing line between GFP-positive and negative cells was set to 1% of the background level in every no-I-SceI control. This gate was then applied to the plus-I-SceI sample to determine HR efficiency. For the RNA polymerase Ш inhibitor experiment, ML-60218 (Calbiochem) were added to a final concentration of 40 μM just before transfection with the empty vector or I-SceI, the same volume of DMSO was used as a control. Cells were also harvested and analyzed two days after I-SceI transfection.

    Hygromycin B sensitivity assay

    U2OS DR-GFP cells were first transfected with either RPC1 siRNA or control siRNA, and after two days, were transfected with an empty vector or an I-SceI expression vector, in which RFP was co-expressed. After two days of I-SceI transfection, cells were trypsinized and counted. 1.5 x105 cells or 0.5 x105 cells were re-plated to 6-well plates. A final concentration of 300 ug/ml Hygromycin B was added 8 hours later. The drug was washed out 2 days later and cells were cultured after another 3 days. The cells were trypsinized and counted. For the RNA polymerase Ш inhibitor experiment, ML-60218 (Calbiochem, Cat# 557403) was added to a final concentration of 40 μM just before transfection with the empty vector or I-SceI, the same volume of DMSO was used as a control. Cells were also trypsinized and replated to 6-well plates. After Hygromycin B treatement, cells were collected and counted. Each sample was counted five times and an average number was used to calculate cell survival rate.
