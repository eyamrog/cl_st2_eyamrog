On the cover: Glioblastoma brain tumors are notorious for their vast inter- and intra-tumoral heterogeneity, complicating the development of effective...Show more
On the cover: Glioblastoma brain tumors are notorious for their vast inter- and intra-tumoral heterogeneity, complicating the development of effective therapeutic strategies. In this issue, Jacob et al. (188204) describe a new protocol for generating and biobanking glioblastoma organoids from resected tumor tissue that recapitulate the histological features, cellular diversity, gene expression, and mutational profiles of their corresponding parental tumors and allow for testing of personalized therapies. The cover image shows an immunostained glioblastoma organoid containing heterogeneous cell populations with diverse cellular and nuclear morphologies, stained with glioma stem cell marker NESTIN (green), glioma stem cell and astrocyte-like cell marker BLBP (red), and nuclei marker DAPI (blue). Image credit: Fadi Jacob.

    Obituary

    • Shinya Inou (19212019)

      • Timothy J. Mitchison
      The mitotic spindle is a huge molecular machine or, alternatively, an organizational state of cytoplasm, which forms transiently during mitosis to segregate chromosomes and position the cleavage furrow. We now know that it is built from microtubules whose polymerization dynamics shape the spindle and generate force to segregate chromosomes. Dynamic spindle fibers were discovered in the pre-molecular era of cell biology by Shinya Inou, a pioneer of cell motility research and live cell microscopy who died in 2019.

    Bench to Bedside

    • Stabilizing HIF to Ameliorate Anemia

      • Richard A. Voit,
      • Vijay G. Sankaran
      Erythropoietin (EPO) production in the kidney is regulated by the oxygen-sensing transcription factor HIF-1, which is degraded under normoxic conditions by HIF-prolyl hydroxylase (HIF-PHD). Inhibition of HIF-PHD by roxadustat leads to increased EPO production, better iron absorption, and amelioration of anemia in chronic kidney disease (CKD).

    Commentary

    • The Master Observational Trial: A New Class of Master Protocol to Advance Precision Medicine

      • Dane Dickson,
      • Jennifer Johnson,
      • Raymond Bergan,
      • Rebecca Owens,
      • Vivek Subbiah,
      • Razelle Kurzrock
      This commentary introduces a new clinical trial construct, the Master Observational Trial (MOT), which hybridizes the power of molecularly based master interventional protocols with the breadth of real-world data. The MOT provides a clinical venue to allow molecular medicine to rapidly advance, answers questions that traditional interventional trials generally do not address, and seamlessly integrates with interventional trials in both diagnostic and therapeutic arenas. The result is a more comprehensive data collection ecosystem in precision medicine.

    • SnapShot: Splicing Alterations in Cancer

      • Mohammad Alinoor Rahman,
      • Adrian R. Krainer,
      • Omar Abdel-Wahab
      RNA splicing, the spliceosome-catalyzed process by which pre-messenger RNA (pre-mRNA) is processed to mature mRNA, is altered in a number of ways in cancer. Tumor-specific splicing alterations are created by mutations that disrupt splicing-regulatory elements within genes and impair splicing recognition or by altering the RNA-binding preferences of individual splicing factors. This SnapShot summarizes our current understanding of splicing-factor alterations in cancers. To view this SnapShot, open or download the PDF.
